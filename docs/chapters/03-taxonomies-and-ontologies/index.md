# Taxonomies and Ontologies

## Summary

This chapter builds the classification vocabulary — controlled vocabularies,
thesauri, and formal ontologies — needed before formalizing concepts as a
knowledge graph. It moves from simple hierarchical taxonomies through
thesaurus relationships (broader, narrower, related terms) to the more
expressive ontology structures (class hierarchies, is-a and part-of
relationships) that later chapters formalize with RDF and OWL. Readers will
finish able to distinguish a taxonomy from a thesaurus from a full ontology,
and know when each is the right tool.
