# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 15 chapters covering 218 concepts.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Graph Fundamentals](01-graph-fundamentals/index.md) - Introduces the core vocabulary of graphs — nodes, edges, and triples — that every later chapter builds on.
2. [Data Storage Paradigms and Graph Variants](02-data-storage-paradigms/index.md) - Contrasts graph databases with relational tables and document stores, then surveys graph variants.
3. [Taxonomies and Ontologies](03-taxonomies-and-ontologies/index.md) - Builds the classification vocabulary needed before formalizing concepts as a knowledge graph.
4. [Graph Data Models](04-graph-data-models/index.md) - Compares RDF triples and property graphs, culminating in a formal definition of "knowledge graph."
5. [Schema Design and Constraints](05-schema-design-constraints/index.md) - Covers classes, properties, cardinality, and constraints for designing a rigorous schema.
6. [Semantic Web Standards](06-semantic-web-standards/index.md) - Introduces RDF, RDFS, OWL, SKOS, and SPARQL alongside URIs and serialization formats.
7. [Building Knowledge Graphs from Data](07-building-knowledge-graphs/index.md) - Walks through the ETL pipeline used to construct a knowledge graph from real-world sources.
8. [Querying Knowledge Graphs](08-querying-knowledge-graphs/index.md) - Teaches SPARQL and Cypher query patterns and the Python client libraries used to run them.
9. [Graph Algorithms and Analytics](09-graph-algorithms-analytics/index.md) - Applies centrality, traversal, and community detection to uncover structural patterns.
10. [Knowledge Graphs and Machine Learning](10-knowledge-graphs-machine-learning/index.md) - Explains how embeddings and graph neural networks enable link prediction and related tasks.
11. [Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models](11-knowledge-graphs-llms/index.md) - Explores how GraphRAG grounds LLM outputs in a knowledge graph to reduce hallucination.
12. [Cross-Industry Applications](12-cross-industry-applications/index.md) - Surveys real-world knowledge graphs across search, biomedical, recommendation, and other industries.
13. [Litigation and Matter Knowledge Graphs](13-litigation-matter-graphs/index.md) - Applies knowledge graph techniques to litigation and matter management, anchored by a real-world worked example.
14. [Contracts, IP, and Legal Standards](14-contracts-ip-legal-standards/index.md) - Covers contract graphs, intellectual property graphs, and the real-world SALI and FOLIO legal ontologies.
15. [Governance and Maintenance](15-governance-and-maintenance/index.md) - Covers keeping a knowledge graph accurate and trustworthy over time.

## How to Use This Textbook

Chapters are ordered so that every concept's prerequisites appear in an
earlier chapter — read them in order the first time through. Chapters 13 and
14 assume a technical foundation from Chapters 1–12; readers whose primary
interest is the legal applications can skim the earlier chapters for
vocabulary and spend more time on 13–14.

---

**Note:** Each chapter includes a list of concepts covered. Make sure to complete prerequisites before moving to advanced chapters.
